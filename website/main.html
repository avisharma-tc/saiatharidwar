About the Temple

🙏
A beacon of faith since 1994

Shri Sai Baba Mandir, Bhupatwala

This is the first Shirdi Sai Baba temple established in the holy city of Haridwar. The land for the Mandir was acquired in September 1990 in the name of Pt. Chunilal, and the Sai Baba Charitable Trust Parashuram Ashram was formed in September 1991.

The foundation was laid the same year, and after three and a half years of devoted construction funded entirely by devotees' donations, the temple was inaugurated on 5th May 1994 by Shri A.K. Tailor of Rishikesh.

Baba's sacred Dhuni was specially brought from Shirdi by Shri Kote Patil, a Trustee of Shri Saibaba Sansthan Trust, Shirdi. For the Pran Pratishtha ceremony, nine Havan Kunds were prepared and a week-long puja was conducted in the Vedic tradition by 51 Acharyas.

"Sabka Malik Ek" — One God governs all. The temple has grown from 5 founder trustees in 1991 to 21 trustees since 1998, and a magnificent 45 kg silver Simhasan was installed in May 2001.

Stay With Us

Sai Dham Dharamshala

Your Home in the Holy City

Affordable, comfortable accommodation right next to Sai Baba Mandir

Sai Dham is a charitable dharamshala operated by the Shri Sai Baba Charitable Trust, located right next to the temple in Bhupatwala, Haridwar. It offers clean, comfortable and affordable rooms for devotees and pilgrims visiting Haridwar for darshan, festivals or the sacred Ganga.

Ideal for families, solo pilgrims, and groups — Sai Dham provides a peaceful, spiritual atmosphere with the convenience of being steps away from the Mandir. Wake up to morning Aarti, spend your day at Har Ki Pauri, and return to the comfort of Baba's abode.

Whether you're visiting for a day or staying for a week during festivals like Ram Navami or Kumbh Mela, Sai Dham ensures your stay is blessed and worry-free.

Celebrations

Events Across the Year

January
Makar Sankranti & New Year Celebrations
Special puja and distribution of prasad as devotees welcome the new year with Baba's blessings. Community Annadanam for the needy.
March / April
Ram Navami — Sai Baba's Birth Anniversary
Grand three-day celebration with elaborate Havan, Bhajan Sandhya, processions, and Maha Prasad. One of the most significant festivals at the temple.
May
Temple Anniversary — Sthapana Divas
Commemorating the inauguration of the temple on 5th May 1994 with week-long special pujas, cultural programs, and community feasts.
July
Guru Purnima
Celebrating the divine Guru with special Abhishekam, Sai Satcharitra Parayan, and devotional music sessions honoring Baba as the eternal Guru.
August / September
Ganesh Chaturthi & Janmashtami
Festive celebrations with Baba's darshan, special decorations, and community prayers. Midnight Janmashtami celebrations with Bhajan programs.
September / October
Vijayadashami — Sai Baba Punyatithi
Solemn remembrance of Baba's Mahasamadhi with three-day observance including Padyatra, Palki procession, and special Aarti sessions.
October / November
Diwali & Kartik Purnima
Temple illuminated with thousands of diyas. Special Lakshmi Puja, fireworks, and distribution of sweets to the community.
Every Thursday
Weekly Sai Baba Vrat & Bhajan
Thursday is Baba's special day — special Aarti, Sai Satcharitra reading, Bhajan-Kirtan, and community Annadanam (free meals) for devotees.

Charitable Activities

Our Seva & Services

🕉️

Daily Aarti & Puja

Four Aartis performed daily — Kakad, Madhyanha, Dhup, and Shej Aarti following the traditional Shirdi Sai Baba rituals. Abhishekam and Holy Bath ceremonies for Baba's idol.

🍲

Annadanam

Free community meals (Bhandara) served to devotees and the needy every Thursday and during festivals, embodying Baba's teaching of service to humanity.

📖

Sai Satcharitra Parayan

Regular readings of the Sai Satcharitra — the holy scripture of Sai Baba's life and teachings. Weekly and special occasion group readings for devotees.

🎵

Bhajan & Kirtan

Devotional music sessions and community singing programs celebrating Baba's name. Special Bhajan Sandhya events during festivals and Thursdays.

🧘

Yoga & Meditation

Classes in Yoga and Meditation conducted at the temple premises for the physical and spiritual wellbeing of the community.

❤️

Charitable Trust Activities

Support for religious, cultural, educational and charitable activities. The trust organizes medical camps, educational programs, and community welfare initiatives.
